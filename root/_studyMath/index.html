How to Study for Mathematics Assessments

By: Matthew M.

Studying for math is seen as an impossible task, but that is just not true. I have compiled a list of things that will help your study/preemptively study for a math test or any test.

Throughout Semester

Despite what many think, studying for an assessment starts at the beginning of the semester. The following is a list of items that will help you study when an assessment appears.

  • Take notes
    Taking notes in class allows you to review information later, instead of trying to remember what the teacher has said.
  • Review your notes
    Your notes will remind you of what you have forgotten.
  • Ask questions & get answers
    If you don't understand something ask, your teachers are here to help you and not to torture you.
  • Take advantage of extra credit
    Mistakes happen, extra credit allows you to have a "buffer" zone of extra points.
  • Find areas you have problems with
    You can't study if you don't know where you are making your mistakes.
  • Practice, practice, practice
    Math is a skill where practice makes perfect. If you don't practice, you will not improve. That's why math teachers assign homework, but sometimes homework is not enough. Ask your teacher for an extra worksheet or look online for extra practice. You can also look at the resources I have provided below.
  • Ask for help, if you need it
    If you can't figure out a problem, ask a teacher for help. That's what they are paid to do.
  • Use resources (see list below)
    I have compiled a list of resources for you to use, so you don't have to search the Internet.

Upcoming Assessment

The following is a list of items that will help you study for an assessment.

  • Ask for the assessment format
    Knowing if your assessment is true/false, multiple choice or short answer will help you understand how to study. For instance, for multiple choice, you know that if you make a mistake, you will probably not find an answer, so you would study to understand everything and not to find where your error are.
  • Look over your notes
    Your notes will remind you of what you have done over the semester.
  • Practice!
    Do math problems, don't just look over your notes.
  • Don't cram
    Studying for an assessment in one or two days with content from a whole semester is impossible. Spread out your study session over multiple days or even weeks. Also, take short breaks if your study session is longer than an hour. Sometimes your brain needs a break from doing math.
  • Get a good night sleep before your assessment (if possible)
    A good night sleep will reinforce what you have studied. According to National Sleep Foundation , a good night's sleep will improve your memory and help you retain information for longer.
  • Don't pull an all nighter
    Goes with the point above. Sleep.org states that all-nighters make it harder to concentrate, make decisions, and delay reaction time. Also, all-nighters worsen your memory.
  • Use resources (see list below)
    Look below; I have compiled resources for you to use.

Resources

If you find an excellent resource that you don't see here, you can email me at website@matthewm.com.pl , and I will review it and consider adding it to the list!

Free

  • Wolfram Alpha
    Wolfram Alpha is useful to check your answer.
  • Wolfram Alpha Problem Generator
    This resource allows you to generate problems to solve.
  • Khan Academy
    Khan Academy has a lot of helpful videos and guided practice to help you through a lot of subjects.
  • Photomath
    Photomath is available for both IOS & Android phones. This resource is useful to check your answers. It will also show you a step by step process on how to solve a problem.

Paid

  • IXL (unless paid by school)
    Despite IXl's lousy reputation with students and rage-inducing practice, IXl is an excellent resource to practice specific math problems.
